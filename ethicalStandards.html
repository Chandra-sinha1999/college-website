Publication Ethics and Malpractice Statement

Vision & Quest International Journal of Science, Technology & Management (VQIJSTM), Publication Ethics and Malpractice Statement is decided by the Advisory Committee and the Editorial Board of the Journal. The duties and the expectations of the reviewers, editors and of the authors of the journal are as follows:

1. Publication information

Publication Frequency : This journal is published bi-annually (June and December).

Cost of publication : No cost of publication is to be incurred by the authors.

Open Access : This journal provides an open access for all the published research papers.

2. Authors' responsibilities

Reporting Standards: The research paper(s) of the author(s) should be original; authentic and must include appropriate facts, figures and data. The paper must contain adequate references and details so that the research work can be further used as reference materials for future studies and research purposes. Presence of inaccurate contents, pirated data, violation of copyright and plagiarism would be considered as unethical and the research paper would be disqualified for acceptance for publication.

Originality and Plagiarism: Authors are required to present their original work which has not been published earlier or has been submitted for publishing, partially or completely. In case there is a mention and quotes of others work, it needs to be cited and due credits are to be given to the concerned person/author whose work has been cited . In case the author wishes to use someone else’s work/ image et al., he must take written permission from the author who holds copyright for the same.

If the research paper is found to be plagiarised, it would not be accepted for publication and would be rejected. Authors are solely responsible for the originality and authenticity of their research paper. Plagiarism of more than 15% is not permissible

In case the ethical guidelines are not followed or deceitful arguments are promoted, it would lead to withdrawal of the published work.

Copyright: The articles which have been published are copyrighted and are the property of the journal. An article can be partially or totally published elsewhere, only after taking due consent from the editors.

Multiple, Redundant or Concurrent Publication: The author must avoid publishing manuscripts which portray similar researches, in either conferences or other journals as this would not be acceptable and would be considered as unethical publishing behaviour.

Acknowledgement of Sources: The work of other publishers needs to be duly acknowledged. The authors must cite only those publications which are relevant to their research activities.

Authorship of the Paper: The authorship of the paper should be limited to the ones who have made a noteworthy contribution towards the concept, design, interpretation and execution of the research/study conducted. Authors who contribute significantly for the research activities are to be listed as the co-authors; and the ones who contributed through certain substantive aspects in the project are to be acknowledged as contributors. Before the submission of the final research paper, the author must ensure that the final edited version of the research paper is approved the co-author(s) and permission for publication of the research paper has been sought. It should be ensured that a proper and active communication between the author and the co-author(s) are to be maintained to smoothen the process of paper publication.

Disclosure and Conflicts of Interest: If there are any financial conflict or conflict of interest, which might influence the results or interpretation of the manuscript, such conflicts are to be disclosed and resolved jointly by all the authors. The sources of the financial support must be disclosed to the editors.

Author Self-Archiving: : Authors are not to publish the articles on any other websites; either personal or of any institution to which they belong to. If need be, the authors may post their paper with due permission from the editors and have to mention with proper indication and details, quoting that the research paper was published in the Vision & Quest International Journal of Science, Technology & Management.

Fundamental errors in published works: In case there is a notable error or mistake identified by the author(s) in their published work , a quick notification must be sent to the editor of the journal and the author(s) must coordinate with the editor in order to make the required corrections in the research paper.

3. Reviewers' Responsibilities

Each paper that has been submitted for publication has to go through a strict double-blind peer-review process, which would be conducted by two reviewers, who are experts in the concerned area of the research. The factors considered for reviewing the paper are as follows:

  • Relevance: Research papers would be considered if found relevant for the study of this journal?
  • Soundness: Paper would be considered if found technically sound, relevant and complete in all aspects.
  • Support:The claims made by author in their research paper must be supported by experimental / theoretical results.
  • Significance: The paper should hold significance for other media and also for the communication researchers?
  • Originality: The paper must portray new ideas and new dimension of research.
  • Readability: The research paper should be easily understandable and well-organized.
  • Language: The paper should be written in English language with error free grammar and correct punctuations

Note: Out of these factors, the most important ones are originality and significance

Contribution to Editorial Decisions: Peer review assists Editor-in-Chief/Editor and the Editorial Board in making editorial decisions and editorial communications with the authors regarding any query or improvement of the paper. VQIJSTM reviewers work for the journal on a volunteer basis.

The decisions of the Editorial Board include acceptance of research paper, acceptance of paper with revisions, revised resubmission, or rejection. Articles that are poorly written or found to be disorganized and do not meet the prescribed standards of the journal would be rejected. In such conditions, the authors would be directed by the editorial board to update and resubmit their research paper, the acceptance of the revised submission is not guaranteed. Rejected articles will not be re-reviewed. Articles can be rejected without being reviewed if the Editor-in-Chief/Editor deems the article not suitable for publication.

Promptness: If a reviewer feels that the received manuscript does not suite the area of his/her specialization and as a result of which the reviewing process will suffer, he/she needs to notify the matter to the editor.

All reviews should be conveyed to the editorial board in the prescribed time. The review of an article should be completed by the reviewers within one month from the date of submission of the manuscript and the paper after reviewing should be sent to the VQIJSTM editorial board for further processing.

Confidentiality: Beside the editor and other persons authorized by the editor, reviewers are not allowed to reveal information or discuss about the articles, research papers and the received manuscripts as they are confidential documents.

The reviewing process should be objective and unbiased. The reviewers should argue their statements logically and must abstain from personal criticism.

The reviewers must utilize the review form that would be provided by the editors along with the submitted article. If the reviewers agree, then their names would be published in the list of former reviewers of the journal, when the reviewers give their consent to do a review of the research article.

In case the answers from previous reviewers are insufficient, inappropriate or do not reach the editor in the prescribed time frame, the editorial board reserves the right to resend a manuscript to additional reviewers.

Standards of Objectivity: Reviews should be conducted objectively. Personal criticism of the author is not acceptable. Referees should express their views clearly with supporting arguments.

Acknowledgement of Sources: Reviewers should identify the published work that has not been cited by the authors. Relevant citation should be mentioned in case if any result or argument has been previously reported. A reviewer should inform the editorial board and draw their attention to any substantial similarity of the received manuscript and any other published paper, of which the reviewer has a personal knowledge.

Interest conflict: One must make sure that the information or ideas which are derived via peer review are kept confidential. Reviewers must not consider the manuscripts that would generate conflicts of interest, are a consequence of collaborative, competitive or some other form of relationships, which may have certain connections with the authors/ institutions/ companies that are connected to publish papers.

Responsibilities of Editor

Editor/ Editor in chief will respond to every submission by giving replies and appropriate comments like, ‘manuscript is rejected’, ‘manuscript is considered for publication’ or ‘manuscript is accepted for publication’. Usually, the article must be processed within three months of submission by the author, and till the date of which the communication of the editor’s last and final decision takes place.

The Editor-in-Chief/Editor of the VQIJSTM has the exclusive authority to decide as which article are to be published from among the submitted manuscripts. Both of them may come under the guidance of the editorial policies part of the journal and they must be constrained by legal requirements. The quality of the publication would be judged by the Editor or the Editor-in-Chief. The Editor would consider and understand the needs of the authors and also the readers while making changes/ improving the quality of the research paper for publication. The editors must make sure that all the research matter published by them confirms to the ethical guidelines accepted internationally by meeting all the aspects of legalities pertaining to the copyright, plagiarism, infringement etc. Editor-in-chief may consult with the other members of Editorial Board or the reviewers in order to arrive to a decision about the quality of paper for publication.

The Editor should either accept the manuscript or reject it based on the scientific content and relevancy of the material and must not consider the gender, nationality, race, institutional affiliation or age of the authors.

Only the manuscripts which are considered for the purpose of publication are meant to be sent for the process of blind peer reviewing. The Editor or Editor-in-Chief would choose the most suitable reviewers taking into consideration the content of the manuscript and the expertise of the reviewer. When the process of peer review takes place, the editors must ensure that the identity of the authors remains hidden.

The editors have the authority to expose the information regarding the manuscript submitted to the corresponding author (s), additional editorial advisors and reviewers only. However an exception to this is made for a manuscript which has been suspected to be submitted twice.

The editor might not pay attention to the matter which breaks the legal demands like copyright, infringement, libel, and plagiarism.

The editors must defend the standards of integrity of the journal. It is also the responsibility of the editor to take into consideration of any misconduct conducted by the reviewers or even the authors. In case of any suspicion about the authenticity of the article, the publication would be delayed till necessary clarification is sought .

The Editor-in-Chief, Editor, the members of the Editorial Board, and any editorial staff must not disclose any information about a submitted manuscript to anyone other person than the authors of the manuscript, reviewers, potential reviewers, other editorial advisers, and the publisher, as appropriate.

4. Disclosure and Conflicts of interest:

Unpublished materials or any submitted manuscripts should not be used for the research purpose of the Editor-in-Chief/Editor or the members of the Editorial Board without the written consent of the author.

5. Publisher Responsibility:

The publisher of VQIJSTM is the sole authority that would have the rights to sell the journal to an individual, agencies and institutions or authorize an agency for marketing the journal. Authors are not allowed to be involved in the financial activities like selling the published papers or the journal. All disputes are subjected to the exclusive jurisdiction of the competent courts and forums in Ghaziabad, Uttar Pradesh, India. All rights are reserved with the publisher of VQIJSTM. No part of the published items of the journal can be reproduced or copied in any form without written permission of the Publisher or Editor. The views expressed by the authors are personal remarks and do not necessarily reflect the views of VQIJSTM or the organization they represent.
